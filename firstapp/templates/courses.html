  • JEE

The Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) in India is a pivotal examination for aspiring engineers. Split into two stages, JEE Main and JEE Advanced, it serves as the gateway to prestigious institutions like the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and National Institutes of Technology (NITs). JEE Main, the initial phase, caters to a broad spectrum of candidates nationwide, acting as a qualifying round for various engineering colleges. JEE Advanced, the subsequent level, is exclusively for top performers in JEE Main, granting access to the coveted IITs. The exam rigorously evaluates candidates' proficiency in physics, chemistry, and mathematics, necessitating a comprehensive understanding of the 10+2 curriculum

  • NEET

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) in India is a standardized entrance examination for admission to undergraduate medical and dental programs in various medical colleges across the country. Introduced in 2013, NEET replaced multiple state and national-level medical entrance exams, unifying the admission process for medical courses. NEET is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) and is mandatory for students seeking admission to MBBS, BDS, and other related courses. The exam assesses candidates' knowledge in physics, chemistry, and biology (botany and zoology). It follows a multiple-choice question format and is conducted annually.
